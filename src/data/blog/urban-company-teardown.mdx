  <section class="uc-section uc-section--problem" id="problem">
    <div class="uc-section-heading">
      <p class="uc-label">01 / Problem</p>
      <h2>The booking engine assumes one actor. Real households often have three.</h2>
    </div>
    <div class="uc-two-col">
      <div>
        <p>
          Urban Company’s core transaction flow is optimized for the simplest case: the person booking the service is also the resident at home, the approver of extra costs, and the payer at completion.
        </p>
        <p>
          That assumption breaks for remote property owners, cross-city professionals, and NRIs managing family homes or rental properties from a distance. Coordination falls back to phone calls, WhatsApp screenshots, ad-hoc confirmations, and awkward cash handoffs.
        </p>
      </div>
      <blockquote class="uc-pullquote">
        Remote property owners manage home services through fragmented manual channels, creating coordination delays, payment friction, and anxiety for everyone on site.
      </blockquote>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="uc-section" id="personas">
    <div class="uc-section-heading">
      <p class="uc-label">02 / Stakeholders</p>
      <h2>The system needs to respect each actor’s job-to-be-done.</h2>
    </div>
    <div class="uc-persona-grid">
      <article class="uc-card">
        <p class="uc-card-label">Primary</p>
        <h3>The Orchestrator</h3>
        <p class="uc-muted">Remote payer: NRI, cross-city owner, or property manager.</p>
        <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Keep the home maintained remotely with financial control and a clean service record.</p>
        <p><strong>Friction:</strong> Timezone gaps, billing opacity, and too many manual updates.</p>
      </article>
      <article class="uc-card">
        <p class="uc-card-label">Secondary</p>
        <h3>The Resident</h3>
        <p class="uc-muted">Local contact: parent, tenant, caretaker, or neighbor.</p>
        <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Let the technician in and get the household problem resolved quickly.</p>
        <p><strong>Friction:</strong> Avoids new apps, payment negotiation, and repeated coordination calls.</p>
      </article>
      <article class="uc-card">
        <p class="uc-card-label">Tertiary</p>
        <h3>The Partner</h3>
        <p class="uc-muted">Service professional executing the job.</p>
        <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Complete work efficiently and protect the day’s earning potential.</p>
        <p><strong>Friction:</strong> Waits at gates, loses time on approvals, and absorbs payment uncertainty.</p>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="uc-principles">
      <h3>Solution principles</h3>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Zero resident software dependency:</strong> the local contact can operate through standard SMS.</li>
        <li><strong>No partner app redesign:</strong> the technician flow remains familiar, with orchestration handled by backend routing.</li>
        <li><strong>Asynchronous payment locks:</strong> small, predictable repair additions should not stall on remote approval.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="uc-section" id="journey">
    <div class="uc-section-heading">
      <p class="uc-label">03 / System Flow</p>
      <h2>A lightweight bridge between intent, access, execution, and payment.</h2>
    </div>
    <div class="uc-flow">
      <article>
        <span>1</span>
        <h3>Delegation setup</h3>
        <p>The remote owner adds a resident name, phone number, relationship, and Smart Buffer threshold during checkout.</p>
      </article>
      <article>
        <span>2</span>
        <h3>SMS dispatch</h3>
        <p>The platform sends booking details, arrival context, and an entry PIN to the resident’s normal mobile number.</p>
      </article>
      <article>
        <span>3</span>
        <h3>Verification</h3>
        <p>The resident shares the offline-safe code with the technician, allowing service start without app installs or internet dependency.</p>
      </article>
      <article>
        <span>4</span>
        <h3>Budget capture</h3>
        <p>Minor additions within the pre-authorized limit are captured instantly. Anything above the limit triggers a hard-stop approval flow.</p>
      </article>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="uc-section" id="pain-points">
    <div class="uc-section-heading">
      <p class="uc-label">04 / MVP Scope</p>
      <h2>Three features are enough to prove the orchestration thesis.</h2>
    </div>
    <div class="uc-feature-stack">
      <article class="uc-feature">
        <div>
          <p class="uc-card-label">F01 / Checkout</p>
          <h3>On-site contact delegation</h3>
        </div>
        <p>Dedicated inputs capture the resident’s details and bind service lifecycle alerts to the physical property location.</p>
      </article>
      <article class="uc-feature">
        <div>
          <p class="uc-card-label">F02 / Payment</p>
          <h3>Pre-authorized Smart Buffer</h3>
        </div>
        <p>A temporary card or UPI mandate lock covers predictable material additions, so small repairs can move without timezone bottlenecks.</p>
      </article>
      <article class="uc-feature">
        <div>
          <p class="uc-card-label">F03 / Approval</p>
          <h3>Asynchronous CapEx hard stop</h3>
        </div>
        <p>When costs exceed the buffer, the partner pauses execution and sends a diagnostic report with photos for remote approval.</p>
      </article>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="uc-section" id="mvp">
    <div class="uc-section-heading">
      <p class="uc-label">06 / Fail-Safes</p>
      <h2>The MVP has to handle the messy parts without pretending they are edge cases.</h2>
    </div>
    <div class="uc-risk-grid">
      <article class="uc-risk">
        <p class="uc-card-label">Timezone gap</p>
        <h3>Owner is asleep during a hard stop</h3>
        <p><strong>Mitigation:</strong> Smart Pause Window saves diagnostic state and schedules an automated return visit within 24 hours.</p>
      </article>
      <article class="uc-risk">
        <p class="uc-card-label">Weak signal</p>
        <h3>Resident cannot receive the PIN</h3>
        <p><strong>Mitigation:</strong> Offline PIN logic allows authorization using deterministic phone-number-based fallback rules.</p>
      </article>
      <article class="uc-risk">
        <p class="uc-card-label">Payment failure</p>
        <h3>Pre-auth capture fails at completion</h3>
        <p><strong>Mitigation:</strong> Grace Period Billing completes the job and sends a secure payment mandate link with a 24-hour window.</p>
      </article>
    </div>
  </section>
